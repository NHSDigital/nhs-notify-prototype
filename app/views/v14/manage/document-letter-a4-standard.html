Dear Mr Max Mustermann

I'm writing to remind you to get your COVID-19 vaccination this spring. This is because either:

  • you're aged 75 or over (by 30 June 2023), or
  • your NHS record suggests you may have a weakened immune system due to a health condition or medical treatment.

It's important you get this further vaccination as soon as possible, as protection fades over time. Your protection may reduce more quickly than other people's does. This vaccination will lower your risk of hospital admission and help you to recover more quickly if you catch COVID-19 during the spring or summer.

The final day to get your spring vaccination is 30 June 2023. Arrange your vaccination as soon as possible if you don't already have an appointment booked.

To book online:

  • Visit www.nhs.uk/get-vaccination or scan the QR code at the top of this invitation.

To book using the NHS App:

  • Log in and choose ‘Book or manage a coronavirus (COVID-19) vaccination'. The app is available at nhs.uk/NHSapp.

To find a walk-in site:

  • You can also go to a walk-in vaccination site without an appointment. Check before you go at www.nhs.uk/vaccine-walk-in.

On the phone:

  • If you can't get online, phone 119 free of charge to book an appointment or find a walk-in site. Someone else can phone for you. If you need an interpreter, please let us know when you call.
  • You can use text phone 18001 119 or the NHS British Sign Language interpreter service at signvideo.co.uk/nhs119.

If you usually get your care at home, contact your GP practice. They should either provide a home vaccination or arrange one with another local NHS service.

At your appointment
If you are aged under 75, please bring this invitation, an NHS letter describing your condition or treatment, or a repeat prescription or medicine box with your name and a recent date. You'll need to speak to someone before vaccination to check that you're eligible.

Pneumonia vaccination
If you're aged 65 or over, or have certain long-term health conditions, you may also be eligible for a one-off pneumonia vaccination if you haven't had it already. If you're eligible, and you're having your COVID-19 vaccination at your GP practice, you may be able to have your pneumonia vaccination at the same time. If it's not possible to have them at the same time you may be able to book a separate appointment with your GP practice. For more information and to check if you're eligible visit www.nhs.uk/conditions/vaccinations/pneumococcal-vaccination.

For more information
Please read the enclosed leaflet before your vaccination. The leaflet is available in other languages and formats at https://qrco.de/NHSpb. Further information on the vaccine is also available at www.nhs.uk/covidvaccination.
